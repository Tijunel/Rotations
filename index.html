What is a rotation?

The mathematical definition for a rotation is:

"Rotation in mathematics is a concept originating in geometry. Any rotation is a motion of a certain space that preserves at least one point." - Wikipedia

3D rotations are generally represented in one of three ways:

  • Euler Vectors
  • Matrices
  • Quaternions

Each of these have their benefits and drawbacks, but the quaternion is the gold standard when it comes to video games.

To avoid unexpected behaviour, we need four dimensions to describe three dimensional rotation. The quaternion does just that, and is visualized on the right.

Leonhard Euler

Euler is a famous mathematician who dipped his hand in the theory of rotation. Two of his major discoveries include the Euler vector and Euler Angles.

Euler noticed that the order in which we apply rotations matters, and therefore devised Euler angles to describe the yaw, pitch, and roll (The rotations must be applied in that order). Although, this form has issues as it may result in Gimbal Lock. The Euler Vector fixed these issues, but were not practical to use.

Sir William Rowan Hamilton

Hamilton is a famous mathematician and physicist that discovered the quaternion (and many other things). Many mathematicians called quaternions "evil" for their unintuitive properties. However, the quaternion become the modern standard for expressing mathematical rotations.

Today, quaternions are used for any computational calculations involving rotations.

Who cares?

Many modern devices use 9-DoF (Acceleration, Gyroscope, Magnetometer) IMUs (Inertial Measurement Units) to compute orientation. This allows us to play motion games, use our phone as a level or compass, and so much more. These IMUs output quaternions that enable us to program video games and more without any worries. Even effects like parallax utilize quaternions.

However, there was a time when IMUs used gimbals to compute orientation. This led to problems as IMUs would suffer from Gimbal Lock. The Apollo 13 mission once suffered from Gimbal Lock, which left astronauts in the dark about their orientation, and forced them to manually correct their rotation. Clearly, rotation is very important to the safety of aircrafts and other vehicles.

Properly understanding the mathematics of rotation can save you many headaches if you work in the gaming, or aircraft industries. More importantly, it could save many lives.
