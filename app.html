Lights!

Well, kind of...

Some of Maxwell's equations for light have a simplified form using quaternions, but he chose to keep them out of the official equations because he has "endeavoured to avoid any process demanding of the reader a knowledge of the Calculus of Quaternions".

Quaternions are also used in the world of quantum mechanics to describe spin and other phenomena of quantum particles. It turns out that the imaginary components of the quaternion have very real consequences in the physics of our universe! In general, the universe likes LOVES to make it as difficult as possible to understand it.

Camera!

In the real world, computer stabilized cameras utilize quaternions to physically stabilize the camera, and smooth the motion of the video with post-processing effects. Another unique feature of quaternions is SLERP (Spherical Linear Interpolation) which enables smoothened rotational motion in videogames and motion capture.

In the game world, "cameras" are used to track the character you are playing with. Quaternions are used to smoothly rotate the camera around the player without concerns of gimbal lock or otherwise.

Action!

The small components in the image are called armatures. Armatures are used to animate movement in CGI, video games, and more. Each armature utilizes quaternions to rotate and translate the position of other armatures.

Clearly, a bunch of quaternions can result in some astounding results!
